## Coursera Courses Data Analysis – Sprint 4 Project
### Project Overview

This project focuses on an exploratory data analysis (EDA) and data visualization of a Coursera courses dataset. The main objective is to understand the structure of the data and analyze how different metrics such as course ratings, student enrollments, certification types, difficulty levels, and organizations interact with each other.

The analysis is performed using mostly Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn.

Questions Addressed in the Analysis:

## Course Exploration

How many courses are in the dataset?

Which courses have the highest number of enrollments?

What are the most common words in course titles?

## Certification Exploration

What certification types are present in the dataset?

How many courses are offered per certification type?

How many students are enrolled per certification type?

What is the relationship between course difficulty and certification type?

## Organization Exploration

How many distinct organizations are in the dataset?

Which are the top 10 organizations by number of courses?

Which organizations have the highest average course rating?

What are the average course ratings for the top 10 organizations by number of courses?

How many students are enrolled in courses offered by the top 10 organizations?

How do the top 10 organizations compare across key metrics (number of courses, ratings, and enrollments)?

## Rating Exploration

What is the distribution of course ratings?

What is the relationship between course ratings and student enrollment?

What is the relationship between course ratings and course difficulty?

What is the relationship between course ratings and certification type?
